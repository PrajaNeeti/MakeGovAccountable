# PrajaNeeti: Vision, Goals, and Intentions

## The Core Philosophy
PrajaNeeti is built on a simple premise: **Accountability does not have to mean constantly getting in the way; it can simply mean keeping an honest record that anyone can look at.** 

We aim to separate civic engagement from political noise. When conversations shift from solving problems to political point-scoring, the original issues fade into the background. PrajaNeeti exists to document what is promised, what is delivered, and what remains pending—creating an irrefutable, public record of governance.

## Primary Goals

### 1. Radically Transparent Record-Keeping
- **The Intention:** To provide an immutable, easily accessible ledger of government activities, spending, and policies.
- **The Execution:** A "Transparency Ledger" that tracks audited financial allocations, public project statuses, and oversight efficiency. Data is presented cleanly, without bias or political spin.

### 2. Elevating Public Discourse
- **The Intention:** To move away from the toxic, fast-paced arguments of traditional social media and return to thoughtful, civic-minded discussion.
- **The Execution:** The "Letters & Discourse" forum. Modeled after traditional "Letters to the Editor," it encourages citizens to submit well-thought-out, factual concerns. The interface uses a "Modern Editorial" aesthetic (serif fonts, stark newspaper contrasts) to visually enforce the seriousness of the conversation.

### 3. De-duplicating Civic Concerns
- **The Intention:** To show citizens that they are not alone in their grievances and to consolidate public willpower.
- **The Execution:** Semantic matching on the submission form. Before a citizen submits a concern, the platform scans for similar existing records or related government context, connecting individual voices into a larger, organized civic record.

### 4. Community-Driven and Open Source
- **The Intention:** To ensure the platform never relies on a single point of failure or a single perspective. No one person can track an entire country.
- **The Execution:** Open-source development. Developers, researchers, journalists, and citizens can contribute data, verify information, and build new features to scale the platform's reach.

## Target Audience
- The citizen who wants to pay closer attention to their city or state without being drowned in political campaigns.
- The researcher or journalist looking for an organized, chronological ledger of government actions and public sentiment.
- The voter who feels unrepresented by the noise and simply wants to know: *Did they do what they said they would do?*

## What PrajaNeeti is NOT
- **Not a campaign tool:** We do not advocate for or against any specific political party.
- **Not a social media feed:** We do not optimize for outrage, endless scrolling, or algorithmic echo chambers. 
- **Not a protest organizer:** We are an information aggregator and a public ledger.
