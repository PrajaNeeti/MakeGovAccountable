# PrajaNeeti: Vision, Goals, and Intentions

## The Core Philosophy
PrajaNeeti is built on a simple premise: **Accountability does not have to mean constantly getting in the way; it can simply mean keeping an honest record that anyone can look at.** 

We aim to separate civic engagement from political noise. When conversations shift from solving problems to political point-scoring, the original issues fade into the background. PrajaNeeti exists to document what is promised, what is delivered, and what remains pending—creating an irrefutable, public record of governance.

---

## Philosophical Foundations

### 1. David Hume: *Of the First Principles of Government* (1742)
> *"As FORCE is always on the side of the governed, the governors have nothing to support them but opinion. 'Tis therefore on opinion only that government is founded."*

Philosopher David Hume observed that physical force ultimately rests with the governed, meaning political authority is sustained entirely by the **opinion** of the public. When public opinion is grounded in manufactured spin or partisan outrage, governance fractures. But when opinion is grounded in **verifiable facts, unspinable records, and open data**, citizens and representatives can engage in productive accountability.

### 2. Chanakya Neeti: The Strength of the State
Chanakya wrote that the strength of a kingdom does not come from its ruler—it comes from its citizens. If people are secure, prosperous, and informed, the state remains stable even through weak leadership.

### 3. Bhagavad Gita: Swadharma & Duty
Krishna reminds Arjuna that it is better to perform your own duty (*swadharma*) imperfectly than someone else's well. Rather than engaging in endless political arguments, PrajaNeeti lets developers, researchers, and citizens perform their duty by building and maintaining open public ledgers.

## Primary Goals

### 1. Radically Transparent Record-Keeping
A public ledger tracking audited financial allocations (such as ₹11,538+ Cr in MPLADS), public project statuses, and oversight efficiency.

### 2. Elevating Public Discourse
The "Letters & Discourse" forum encourages citizens to submit well-thought-out, factual concerns using a stark "Modern Editorial" newspaper aesthetic to visually enforce the seriousness of the conversation.

### 3. De-duplicating Civic Concerns
Semantic matching on the submission form scans for similar existing records before a citizen submits a concern, connecting individual voices into a larger, organized civic record.

### 4. Community-Driven and Open Source
Ensures the platform never relies on a single point of failure. Developers, researchers, journalists, and citizens can contribute data, verify information, and build new features to scale the platform's reach.

---

## What PrajaNeeti is NOT
- **Not a campaign tool:** We do not advocate for or against any specific political party.
- **Not a social media feed:** We do not optimize for outrage, endless scrolling, or algorithmic echo chambers. 
- **Not a protest organizer:** We are an information aggregator and a public ledger.
